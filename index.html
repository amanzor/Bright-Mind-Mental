About Anna

A practice grounded in presence, not pressure.

Anna Rondon is a Licensed Mental Health Counselor and National Certified Counselor based in the Miami area, with telehealth services serving clients across the state of Florida. Her practice is built on a simple conviction: real change rarely arrives in a single leap. It comes in small, deliberate steps — taken with someone who sees you clearly and walks alongside you.

With clinical experience spanning forensic psychology and juvenile services, Anna brings a deep understanding of diverse behavioral and emotional challenges. She is especially passionate about working with adolescents, while also providing therapeutic support to children, adults, and families navigating high-risk seasons of life.

Children & Adolescents: Developmentally Attuned Care

Purpose: This therapeutic approach is specially designed for the unique developmental needs of children and teenagers. It recognizes that kids and teens process emotions, relationships, and challenges differently than adults.

Focus Areas:

  • Emotional development and expression in age-appropriate ways
  • School-related stress, academic pressures, and learning challenges
  • Peer relationships, bullying, and social integration
  • Family dynamics and parent-child communication
  • Identity formation, self-esteem, and belonging
  • Behavioral challenges and impulse control

Why it Matters: Early intervention during childhood and adolescence can prevent patterns from becoming entrenched. This is Anna's primary specialty, drawing on her deep experience with young people.

Adults in Transition: Finding Clarity in Life's In-Between Seasons

Purpose: Life transitions are disorienting. Whether it's a career shift, relationship change, identity exploration, or life stage transition, adults need a space to think deeply and find solid ground.

Focus Areas:

  • Career transitions, job dissatisfaction, and professional identity
  • Relationship uncertainty, dating, partnership challenges
  • Identity exploration and personal authenticity
  • Life purpose and meaning-making
  • Grief and loss during transitions
  • Building resilience through change

Why it Matters: Transitions are not emergencies—they're invitations to understand yourself more deeply. This approach honors the complexity of adult life without rushing the process.

Family Dynamics: Restoring Connection and Trust

Purpose: Families are complex systems. When communication breaks down or old patterns create distance, family therapy helps restore understanding, heal ruptures, and rebuild the foundation of trust.

Focus Areas:

  • Parent-child conflict and communication breakdowns
  • Sibling relationships and family roles
  • Blended family challenges and adjustment
  • Generational patterns and inherited dynamics
  • Grief and loss as a family system
  • Rebuilding trust after rupture or betrayal

Why it Matters: Families are where we learn to relate. Healing family relationships creates ripples that affect all areas of life—work, friendships, romantic relationships, and sense of belonging.

High-Risk Support: Crisis, Trauma, and Complex Challenges

Purpose: Some seasons of life are darker and more complex. High-risk support provides specialized, trauma-informed care for individuals navigating crisis, complex trauma, behavioral challenges, and the aftermath of loss.

Focus Areas:

  • Crisis intervention and stabilization
  • Trauma processing and healing from complex or ongoing trauma
  • Suicidal ideation and self-harm behaviors
  • Behavioral challenges and conduct difficulties
  • Substance use and addiction concerns
  • Forensic and juvenile justice system involvement

Why it Matters: High-risk seasons require specialized understanding. Anna's background in forensic psychology and juvenile services means she brings both clinical expertise and deep compassion to the darkest, hardest moments.

We specialize in treating

Anxiety

Definition: Excessive worry, fear, or nervousness that persists and interferes with daily functioning. Anxiety disorders involve anticipating future threats and can manifest physically and mentally.

Common Symptoms:

  • Persistent worry or racing thoughts
  • Physical tension, restlessness, or trembling
  • Sleep disturbances and concentration difficulties
  • Panic attacks or sudden intense fear
  • Avoidance of triggering situations

Depression

Definition: A persistent mood disorder characterized by deep sadness, loss of interest in activities, and a pervasive sense of hopelessness. Depression affects how individuals think, feel, and function daily.

Common Symptoms:

  • Persistent sadness, emptiness, or numbness
  • Loss of interest in previously enjoyed activities
  • Fatigue, low energy, and sleep changes
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Feelings of worthlessness or guilt

ADHD

Definition: Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder is a neurodevelopmental condition affecting attention, impulse control, and executive functioning. It impacts organization, time management, and emotional regulation.

Common Symptoms:

  • Difficulty sustaining attention or focusing
  • Impulsive decision-making or interrupting others
  • Difficulty organizing tasks and managing time
  • Restlessness, fidgeting, or excessive talking
  • Forgetfulness and difficulty following instructions

Stress

Definition: The body's response to demands or threats. While some stress is normal, chronic stress overwhelms coping mechanisms and impacts physical and mental health significantly.

Common Symptoms:

  • Physical tension, headaches, or muscle aches
  • Rapid heartbeat, difficulty breathing, or chest tightness
  • Irritability, mood swings, or emotional overwhelm
  • Sleep disruption and appetite changes
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions

Mood Disorders

Definition: Conditions characterized by significant disturbances in emotional state, including depression, bipolar disorder, and cyclothymia. They affect overall emotional regulation and life functioning.

Common Symptoms:

  • Persistent sadness, emptiness, or elevated mood
  • Extreme mood swings or cycling between states
  • Changes in energy, sleep, and motivation
  • Difficulty maintaining relationships or work performance
  • Thoughts of hopelessness or worthlessness

Personality Disorders

Definition: Enduring patterns of inner experience and behavior that deviate from cultural expectations, causing distress or impairment. They affect how individuals relate to others and perceive themselves.

Common Symptoms:

  • Pervasive patterns of thinking, feeling, or behaving
  • Difficulty in relationships and social functioning
  • Rigid or maladaptive coping strategies
  • Emotional dysregulation or instability
  • Limited insight into impact on others

Emotional Regulation

Definition: The ability to manage and respond to emotional experiences in healthy ways. Difficulties with emotional regulation involve struggling to control, understand, or express emotions appropriately.

Common Symptoms:

  • Intense or overwhelming emotional responses
  • Difficulty identifying or naming emotions
  • Rapid mood shifts or emotional volatility
  • Impulsive behaviors driven by emotions
  • Difficulty calming down or self-soothing

Adolescent Identity

Definition: The process of developing a sense of self during teenage years. Adolescent identity concerns involve struggles with self-concept, belonging, and navigating peer relationships and personal values.

Common Symptoms:

  • Uncertainty about personal values or beliefs
  • Concern about peer acceptance and fitting in
  • Exploring sexual orientation or gender identity
  • Conflict between personal desires and expectations
  • Self-consciousness or social anxiety

Family Conflict

Definition: Persistent disagreements, communication breakdowns, or relational stress within family systems. Family conflict can involve parent-child tension, sibling rivalry, or generational differences affecting family functioning.

Common Symptoms:

  • Frequent arguments or heated disagreements
  • Poor communication and misunderstandings
  • Emotional distance or withdrawal from family
  • Difficulty resolving differences or compromising
  • Impact on individual emotional health and well-being

Behavioral Challenges

Definition: Difficulties with behavior control, impulse management, or conduct that interferes with functioning at home, school, or in social settings. Often rooted in emotional, developmental, or environmental factors.

Common Symptoms:

  • Aggression, defiance, or oppositional behavior
  • Difficulty following rules or respecting authority
  • Impulsive or reckless decision-making
  • Negative peer interactions or social conflicts
  • Consequences in academic or home environments
The Approach

Small steps. Real ground.

  • We meet

    An introductory conversation to understand what brought you here and what you're hoping for. Fit matters — and there's no pressure to continue.

  • We listen, fully

    Before any framework or technique, the work begins with being heard. Without rush. Without a script. Without judgment.

  • We map a path

    Together, we set goals that feel honest and reachable — drawing from evidence-based modalities including CBT, DBT, Functional Family Therapy, Systems Theory, attachment-based work, and trauma-informed care.

  • We walk it

    Therapy isn't a destination — it's a steady practice of returning to yourself, building insight, resilience, and emotional well-being. One baby step at a time.
